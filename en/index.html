About Burrito Project Montreal

The Montreal chapter of The Burrito Project is a grassroots initiative to build community and feed those who are hungry and/or living in the streets of Montreal. We believe that food should be a secure resource for all, and that no one should have to go hungry in our community.

We deliver by bike and on foot because we believe that issues of space and (im)mobility are significant for all of us! Bicycles are affordable, environmental, practical, and engage with a community in a way that is lost while inside the closed off space of a car.

The Burrito Project practices anti-oppression politics, meaning we will not tolerate any racist, sexist, ableist, classist, ageist, homophobic, transphobic, or any other discriminatory/bigoted comments or actions. If any of our volunteers feel uncomfortable at any point in their time with us, we encourage them to approach us, and we will immediately address the issue. We want our volunteers to have a good time, so we can all work together to fight food insecurity.

The Burrito Project Montréal is a non-profit, with no participant drawing a salary from the endeavour, with all money from sales going back into the project.

Volunteering

There are lots of ways to help out! Cooking up burritos, delivering on bike (or foot!), outreach, etc.

We realise that not everyone can make a long term commitment to volunteering, or are organised enough to plan ahead, so we are always open to volunteers coming along when they can. Of course, it is always appreciated when volunteers can keep on coming back.

Volunteers should wear comfortable, yet full covered, shoes. Delivery volunteers please bring a bag that can hold up to 20 burritos. Gloves, hair coverings and aprons are provided, for use in the kitchen.

When & Where

We prepare our burritos in a down town location near Guy Concordia metro, starting around 12:00 mid-day on Sundays.

Calendar

Check out calendar to see when we are getting together. Typically we are closed for the summer and Christmas, due to organisational constraints.

Catering

Burrito Project Montreal offers catering as a way to raise funds for the project. Burritos are sold for $3 a piece (or to be cut in half as two demi-burritos) for general audiences, and we offer reduced rates for other groups concerned with social justice issues (e.g. tuition hikes, internet censorship, housing/food (in)security, etc.).

Burritos can usually be heated up and delivered. Heating and delivery depends on how much advance notice is given. We generally need at least 10 days prior notice for any catering.
